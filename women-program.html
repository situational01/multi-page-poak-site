Women's Economic Empowerment

Transforming lives through savings, skills, and solidarity

About the Program

Our Women's Economic Empowerment program uses the proven Village Savings and Loan Association (VSLA) model to help women build financial independence. Women form groups, pool their savings, and provide small loans to each other to start or expand businesses.

What We Offer

  • VSLA Groups - Women save together and access small loans
  • Business Training - Financial literacy, marketing, and business planning
  • Startup Support - Seed capital and equipment for new businesses
  • Market Linkages - Connecting women to markets and customers
  • Leadership Development - Training women to lead and mentor others

How VSLA Works

1

Form a Group

15-25 women meet weekly, each contributing a small amount to a shared fund.

2

Save Together

Savings accumulate over 9-12 months, typically 100-500 KES per week.

3

Access Loans

Members can borrow from the group fund at low interest.

4

Share Profits

At year end, profits are shared equally among all members.
